Elouise Eftos: the 10 funniest things I have ever seen (on the internet)

Date : 2024-10-16T14:00:48Z   |   Auteur : Elouise Eftos   |   Journal : Culture

I was aware of the internet from quite a young age, maybe six or seven years old. To be honest, it used to scare the shit out of me – I guess it still does – but that was probably because the first videos I ever saw were of people bulging their eyes out of their sockets and that 3D baby dancing to the Macarena.

I do have to thank the scary world wide web for a lot though: it’s probably why I eventually gave acting and comedy a real go! My obsession with comedy became much stronger because of it; I probably can recite every Laugh Factory standup clip or SNL sketch word for word. But it also broadened my taste in comedy – how else would I have found some of the most absurd, silly, chaotic videos from TV shows we couldn’t get in Australia? I also love that you can just discover hilarious raw talent from around the world at your fingertips (I believe the lower the quality the better) and share it with the people you love to make them laugh (ew sorry, is this too sentimental?). You also might know of me because of the silly videos I’ve made and I genuinely want to say thanks for watching. Instagram is basically my résumé now.

I’d like to think that after 25 years of watching funny things on the internet, I’m quite the expert but GOD has it been hard to narrow this list down to just 10. Ten?? Of the FUNNIEST things I’ve EVER seen on the internet in MY WHOLE LIFE??? What a sick and twisted task the Guardian has given me. But I’m always up for a challenge. Also disclaimer: if you don’t find things on this list funny, that’s on you! Grow up!

1. Charlize Theron on Between Two Ferns with Zach Galifianakis

Everyone surely has seen Between Two Ferns, which was one of the first video series on Funny or Die about 15 years ago. If you haven’t seen any, it’s Zach Galifianakis interviewing celebrities on a low budget set. It’s the perfect level of cringe and makes you question “is this real or scripted?” (It’s actually all improv.) This episode with Charlize Theron is my favourite and I rewatch it every couple of months. She’s so hot and so funny. Zach’s reactions to her flirting with him always make me cackle.

2. ‘The James Bond chord’

The northern English accent, the improvised explanation of a made up Bond scene (if it was directed by Guy Ritchie) and the perfect sound at the end. It’s so satisfying and makes me cackle every bloody time.

3. Willy Wonka & The Chocolate Factory Sweded (original 1971)

OK, so this might be a little niche but: in 2008 Jack Black and Mos Def starred in a film called Be Kind Rewind, in which they worked at a declining VHS rental shop. An accident happens and all the VHS tapes are erased so, in order to save the store, the two men recreate the movies themselves. They call them “Sweded” films because they pretend they came from Sweden. (It’s kind of reminiscent of the “Blockblister” sketches from The Amanda Show.) Anyway, for the release of Be Kind Rewind, they held a Sweded film festival and people entered their Sweded versions of films. I don’t even know how I found this Willy Wonka one years ago, but it’s one of the silliest parodies I’ve ever seen and I rewatch it every chance I get!

4. Saturday Night Live: The Actress

God I love SNL. There are so many sketches and digital shorts that I could put on this list but then this list would be the 10 Funniest Things I’ve Ever Seen on SNL. The Actress is easily one of the silliest and most well-acted shorts and it never gets old. I did my research and I know that it’s on a few people’s lists but you’ll have to get over it, it’s on mine now too.

Emma Stone is perfection in everything she does and has such good acting chops. I also love anything that makes fun of the wanky world of acting. I can say that, I’m an actor!

5. Danger 5

When I first saw a clip of Danger 5 on YouTube, it blew my mind. Made by some very talented film-makers from Adelaide, the series follows five international spies and is set in a campy, bizarre 1960s interpretation of the second world war. I’m obsessed with anything made in the 1960s and I am so impressed by how they’ve made it look like the series came straight out of that era. Such attention to detail and so silly, fun and out there.

6. ‘Nancy Jo, this is Alexis Neiers calling’

Before I explain this video, you may need a quick history lesson: the Bling Ring was a group of teenagers and young adults who broke into celebrities’ homes between October 2008 and August 2009. It was a wild scandal that had a lot of us in a chokehold (including director Sofia Coppola, who made a film about them). One of the members, Alexis Neiers, got herself a reality TV show called Pretty Wild, which starred her, her sisters and their ex-lingerie model mum. While filming the pilot, Alexis was arrested for her involvement in robbing Orlando Bloom’s mansion. (I can only imagine how stoked the producers were.)

This clip is one of the most iconic, unscripted reality TV moments in history and I quote it constantly. The edit alone sends me into orbit. Her excitement at the Vanity Fair article; the disdain when she realises she’s been portrayed in a negative light; the jump cuts every time she has to re-record the voicemail to the journalist; her correcting the brand of heels she actually wore; her mum screaming over her while wearing a Juicy Couture tracksuit. Millennial girls and gays can probably recite it word for word.

7. Alvaro Chavez – me if I was married to Steven Spielberg

I think this was the second video that I saw Alvaro Chavez post and it sits in that very specific niche sense of humour that makes me and a lot of my acting/comedy friends piss our pants. I have no doubt that this is a very accurate insight into what it must be like to be married to Steve: “Are you making a movie today? Are people going to go see it?”

8. Female BBC reporter pinches men’s bums in 1970

Not only is this BBC news clip from 1970 very funny, but it is also very cute and the men’s reactions are soooo British. What a feminist icon Nicky Woodhead is, being brave enough to go out on the street and conduct this cheeky experiment to prove that there isn’t enough equality between the sexes. Ahead of her time! Bring back real journalism!

9. John Early’s Showgirls parody

I love everything John Early does. He is one of the funniest comedians of our generation. This parody of a scene from my favourite cult film Showgirls – which he filmed to promote one of his live shows – is funny but also impressively accurate. If you don’t believe me, there is a video comparing this parody to the actual scene and it’s spooky how accurate it is! The video also features two of John’s friends and colleagues, Kate Berlant and Cole Escola, who I’m also a big fan of. Anything these three do is hilarious – do yourself a favour and watch all their videos.

10. Big Vincenz: Ethnics at the Church

As a second generation Greek-Macedonian girl, ethnic or wog humour has a very special place in my heart; I was a big fan of Fat Pizza, Effie and Superwog. Big Vincenz is my latest obsession on Instagram: he creates some of the silliest videos I’ve ever seen that perfectly encapsulate the wog experience. This video about ethnics at church is so eerily accurate, it’s actually biting social commentary. I lose it every time I watch any of his videos.
