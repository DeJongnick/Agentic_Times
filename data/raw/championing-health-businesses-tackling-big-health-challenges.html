Championing Health: businesses tackling big health challenges

Date : 2025-10-06T19:25:47Z   |   Auteur : Auteur inconnu   |   Journal : Telstra Best of Business Awards: 2025

These Australian small and medium-sized businesses are finding innovative ways to tackle health challenges. Get to know the pioneers in fields from menopause to end-of-life care that have been named State Winners in the Championing Health category of the 2025 Telstra Best of Business Awards.

WELLFEMME – Australian Capital Territory winner

Delivering menopause healthcare to those who previously had limited access, WELLFEMME delivers menopause healthcare via a telehealth clinic. Patients can receive personalised treatment and support through video consultations, and access online resources, to help them navigate the effects of perimenopause and menopause.

　

Preparing the Way – New South Wales winner

Preparing the Way’s team of doulas is changing the way Australians are experiencing diagnoses, ageing, death and grief. Since 2015, the business has trained more than 2,400 end-of-life doulas, who support the ageing and the dying, and their families, through the many stages of the end of life.

　

Pathfinders NT – Northern Territory winner

Pathfinders NT is a social enterprise focused on uncovering the next generation of leaders. By delivering youth wellbeing programs, mentoring and employment pathways to support young people facing disadvantage, disability and trauma, it aims to build confidence, community and a bright future.

Chatstat – Queensland winner

Operating with young people in mind, this social enterprise helps schools and families detect and manage online risks. Using artificial intelligence to track public posts, Chatstat delivers real-time alerts about cyberbullying, hate speech and self-harm, supporting early, compassionate intervention without invading privacy.

　

Occupational Therapy for Children (OTFC) – South Australian winner

Also a winner in the Building Communities category, OTFC redefines occupational therapy in South Australia and beyond by harnessing the power of play. Purpose-built facilities help support kids with varying diagnoses, behavioural concerns or developmental delays, improving the lives of thousands of young Australians.

　

Savu Saunas – Tasmanian winner

Bringing premium sauna and cold plunge therapy to the public across regional and urban Tasmania, Savu Saunas installs and operates architecturally built, Scandinavian-style recovery facilities that are wheelchair accessible and inclusive. The saunas are designed for a communal and social experience for up to eight people, and women-only hours are available.

　

Rahma Health – Victorian winner

Also a State Winner in the Accelerating Women category, Rahma Health creates culturally and psychologically safe health and parenting resources for Arabic-speaking communities. With a focus on families and people affected by war, displacement, racism, poverty and intergenerational trauma, it offers a community-led approach to healthcare from its team of doctors, dentists, midwives and nurses.

Women & Infants Research Foundation – Western Australian winner

　

The Women & Infants Research Foundation is on a mission to transform health outcomes for women, babies and families. Its research informs policy, improves clinical care and delivers measurable outcomes in Australia and globally.

　

Congratulations to all the State Winners working hard to improve health outcomes for Australians.
