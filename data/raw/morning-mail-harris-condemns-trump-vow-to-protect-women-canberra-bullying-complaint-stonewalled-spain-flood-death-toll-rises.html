In-depth

Long before the colonisation of Australia, Indigenous cultural burning practices played a major role in reducing the intensity of bushfires in south-east Australia. But since the arrival of Europeans shrub cover has continued to increase to dangerous levels.

A new study published in the journal Science argues that the “wide-scale reintegration” of cultural burning practices is now needed once again as Australia faces the escalating climate crisis.

Not the news

Few animals fascinate humans as powerfully as whales, but Dr Joe Roman has spent three decades fixated on one specific aspect of their remarkable lives: their poo.

As he reveals, whale faeces can tell us not just what the ocean giants are eating but also a lot about their hormones, stress levels and ocean pollution – everything from microplastics to parasite loads.
