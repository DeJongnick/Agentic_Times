Queensland government to fuel state’s transition to renewable energy with $26bn investment

Date : 2024-06-05T15:00:26Z   |   Auteur : Andrew Messenger   |   Journal : Australia news

The Queensland premier, Steven Miles, is expected to announce today a pledge in next week’s budget to spend $26bn on renewable energy.

It would be the largest ever investment in green power for the sunshine state, and is about $7bn more than previously earmarked.

The funds will go to renewable power, storage and transmission, including:

  • $16.5bn towards renewable energy and storage projects

  • $8.5bn to build a “SuperGrid”, including the CopperString project and renewable energy zones

  • $500m for network batteries and support of local grid solutions

  • $192m for the transmission and training hubs in Townsville and Gladstone.

Miles is expected to make the commitment in a speech at the XXXX brewery for Queensland Day.

About $8.68bn is slated for the next financial year alone.

“One thing I’m particularly passionate about is our state’s transition to renewables, and the economic opportunity that transition can bring,” Miles said.

“It means more jobs for Queensland, more investment in Queensland and more industry innovation right here in Queensland.”

Queensland has legislated a 75% carbon reduction target by 2035.

It has also aimed at an 80% renewable target.

Both are enshrined in law.

The state is also one of the Australia’s most fossil-fuel dependent.

Miles’ speech on Thursday is expected to chart a course beyond fossil fuels for the state.

The state budget, which will be delivered on Tuesday, also includes a reduction in public transport fares to 50 cents, and a free lunch program for primary schoolchildren.
