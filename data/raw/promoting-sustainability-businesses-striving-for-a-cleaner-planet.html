Promoting Sustainability: businesses striving for a cleaner planet

Date : 2025-10-06T19:30:09Z   |   Auteur : Auteur inconnu   |   Journal : Telstra Best of Business Awards: 2025

Sustainable businesses know that the environment must be a top priority. Ranging from a cellar door with a hatted restaurant to a business offering large-scale power solutions, these organisations are dedicated to doing things differently.

Get to know the State Winners in the Promoting Sustainability category of the 2025 Telstra Best of Business Awards.

Big Bag Recovery – Australian Capital Territory winner

Big Bag Recovery repurposes large plastic packaging from the manufacturing, agriculture and construction industries, turning industrial waste into valuable resources and keeping it out of landfill to minimise harm to the environment. This ACT double winner was also awarded in the Embracing Innovation category.

WASHBOX – New South Wales winner

　

WASHBOX is redefining what it means to be clean on a construction site. Its innovative closed-loop wash station for trade tools reduces harmful liquid waste pollution while saving water and helping the construction sector meet global sustainability standards.

　

NAKIE – Queensland winner

Motivated by their concern about plastic pollution in the ocean, the keen scuba divers behind NAKIE dreamt up a way to repurpose this waste. The business started with a 100% recycled hammock made from 37 reused plastic bottles; now it offers numerous thoughtful products, from beach towels to backpacks, that inspire people to get outside in nature.

Teakle Wines – South Australian winner

Teakle Wines is a premium wine, hospitality and tourism business in Port Lincoln. Dedicated to sustainable practices, the business has a carbon footprint action plan, employs water, waste and energy efficiency strategies, and is a certified sustainable winegrower and tourism operator.

　

Enginuity Power Solutions – Tasmanian winner

Tasmania’s Promoting Sustainability winner is an electrical engineering consultancy that helps large-scale energy users reduce electricity consumption. Enginuity Power Solutions uses data-driven, tailored solutions to deliver energy optimisation strategies in commercial and industrial sectors nationwide.

　

Bridgeford Group – Victorian winner

Bridgeford Group is an energy efficiency company that partners with organisations on their journey to achieve net zero. The business provides end-to-end services covering energy efficiency, electrification, asset optimisation and sustainable building design.

　

Li-ion Energy – Western Australian winner

Also a winner in the Embracing Innovation category, Li-ion Energy is an engineering and battery reuse company tackling the environmental challenge of lithium battery waste. The business began in a garage and has grown into a specialised operation focused on lithium battery pack reconditioning, technical dismantling and clean energy system design.

　

Congratulations to all the State Winners reducing their environmental impact and promoting sustainability.
