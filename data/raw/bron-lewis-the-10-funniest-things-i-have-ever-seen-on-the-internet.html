Bron Lewis: the 10 funniest things I have ever seen (on the internet)

Date : 2025-01-29T14:00:09Z   |   Auteur : Bron Lewis   |   Journal : Stage

I’m Bron Lewis, standup comedian, retired high school teacher and knackered mother of three mongrels. And yeah, I’ve seen the internet.

I have had a love-hate relationship with the world wide web since the dial-up days. It’s where I discovered the cringey ways people achieve fame (hello “Leave Britney Alone” guy), but it has also delivered content to me that has seen me crossing my legs tighter than a woman who ran out of her contraceptive pill.

I’m 38 years old and I have an avoidant personality, so I’ve seen more of the internet than I care to admit. So when I was asked to list my top 10 things on the internet, I thought it would be easy. But it wasn’t. Here are things that never fail to make me laugh no matter how many trauma flashbacks I’m having from my time teaching year 8s. I hope there is something in here that tickles your fancy.

1. Saturday Night Live: Liza Minnelli tries to turn off a lamp

Now this clip is one of the ultimate examples of relentless physical comedy, my FAVOURITE kind of comedy. I’m quite a physical comedian myself, so I really appreciate when a comedian leans into how silly their body can be. It’s performed by the legendary Kristen Wiig, who struggles to turn off lamps. It is so outrageous and gets funnier the longer you watch. Pure comedy gold.

2. Chris Parker – checking out of an Airbnb

Everything Chris Parker does cracks me up. I discovered him online, and when I went to see him live I was worried he wouldn’t be as funny as the internet promised, BUT THE INTERNET NEVER LIES! He’s HILARIOUS! I think of this clip every single time I go to an Airbnb, and now you will too. Follow him, then go see him live. You’re welcome.

3. Kate Dolan: ‘Every dance movie in the 2000s’

Kate Dolan is a simply phenomenal standup comedian. The energy this woman brings to absolutely every single gig and silly clip she makes for the internet is unparalleled. Give Kate a follow online, but if you truly respect yourself, go see her live if you get the chance. Thank me later.

4. Zachariah Porter – ‘Every Crafty Mom’

It’s hard to find a clip this man makes that doesn’t make me howl with laughter, but for nostalgia purposes I chose the first one I saw. Zachariah does a number of hilarious characters but the mum character is my ultimate favourite because it is so utterly unhinged.

5. Aaron Chen’s 2021 Melbourne comedy gala set

I think I have watched this set about 500 times. The bit about him trying to describe what apple crumble is to his first-generation Chinese dad when he was only four years old destroys me. It’s so adorable and gives you a whole new appreciation for whatever the hell “crumble” is.

6. Farting on dogs

This one is extremely lowbrow but had my sister and me on the floor crying with laughter. It does exactly what it says on the box: a bloke wandering around a dog park, asking to pat people’s dogs, then farting on them. The best bit is the reactions from the dog owners as the farter casually walks away. The clip isn’t short, and you’d think the novelty would wear off after a few toots, but it actually gets more hilarious as it goes on.

7. Damien Power on his dad’s attitude to life

Damien Power is in every Australian comedian’s top three. His brain works differently to most people’s, which means his jokes are next level. His criticism of society – in this case, a generational inability to communicate feelings – is bang on. If you ever get to see him live, you’ll never forget it.

8. This Lady Gaga fan

This clip is a cautionary tale. If you’re at a music concert, and the artist asks you if you want to come up on stage, say no. At a massive show in Japan, Lady Gaga invited some diehard fans up on stage to help her sing her song Stay. The first girl was able to hold her own, but the second girl sounded like she was gargling a strawberry milkshake at the same time. I hope she’s OK and that she knows she has brightened my day many, many times.

9. Scottish girls get into trouble with their mum

This clip is an OLDIE but a GOLDIE. The two young sisters filming themselves sing and dance seems like an innocent and relatable moment, but when their gruff Scottish mother bursts in and demands to know who didn’t flush the toilet it becomes perfection. The looks on their little faces as they deny the rogue turd is hilarious, and we all watch this remembering that no matter how good life gets our mothers are always ready to humiliate us.

10. This compilation of a guy spooking his wife

Scare videos are my family’s love language. We don’t share feelings, just scare videos, and I wouldn’t have it any other way. This account is excellent because you can see just how fed up she is with her husband. He has had to weigh up what was more important: the marriage or the joke. Fortunately for every divorce lawyer out there, the joke always wins.
