Embracing Innovation: businesses improving Australia’s future

Date : 2025-10-06T19:26:49Z   |   Auteur : Auteur inconnu   |   Journal : Telstra Best of Business Awards: 2025

Driving Australia forward requires changing the way things have previously been done. These small and medium-sized businesses are innovating to improve their fields. Here are the State Winners in the Embracing Innovation category of the 2025 Telstra Best of Business Awards.

Big Bag Recovery – Australian Capital Territory winner

Big Bag Recovery repurposes large plastic packaging from the manufacturing, agriculture and construction industries. By turning industrial waste into valuable resources, it reduces landfill, helps create a circular economy and minimises harm to the environment. This government-accredited business is also a State Winner in the Promoting Sustainability category.

Clinrol – New South Wales winner

　

Harnessing artificial intelligence, Clinrol matches patients to relevant clinical trials to potentially improve their condition and quality of life. Its technology identifies and screens eligible participants and connects them with the right research teams.

　

Practical Risk Solutions NT – Northern Territory winner

Practical Risk Solutions NT is a work health and safety consultancy that provides guidance and training in risk management and safety obligations for businesses across the Northern Territory – even in high-risk and remote environments. Its mission is to provide tailored solutions that enhance worker wellbeing and ensure everyone goes home safely at the end of the day.

　

Comtrac – Queensland winner

Comtrac is a purpose-built platform that harnesses AI to streamline crime investigation, and give hours back to workers on the frontline. Transforming how law enforcement conducts investigations and manages evidence, Comtrac’s tech drives operational efficiency and simplifies complex workflows.

　

SKOOT Community Access – South Australian winner

This business believes community access and transport should be reliable and dignified for people living with a disability. Removing barriers to transport, SKOOT Community Access offers fair pricing, a 24/7 on-demand service and NDIS-friendly options for social outings, medical appointments, work, education and more.

　

KNOWBY – Tasmanian winner

This software company was founded to help businesses create easy-to-follow instructions. KNOWBY helps teams in manufacturing, agriculture, utilities and offices reduce training costs, improve safety and deliver consistent performance.

　

StoryBox Hub – Victorian winner

Created to get children excited about stories and reading, this subscription-based educational platform gives kids the opportunity to see tales being read aloud by engaging Australians. StoryBox Hub also offers a range of resources to help improve language and literacy skills.

　

Li-ion Energy – Western Australian winner

Li-ion Energy is an engineering and battery reuse company tackling the environmental challenge of lithium battery waste. Also a winner in the Promoting Sustainability category, this business began in a garage and has grown into a specialised operation focused on lithium battery pack reconditioning, technical dismantling and clean energy system design.　

Congratulations to all the State Winners embracing innovation to solve the challenges faced by modern Australia.
