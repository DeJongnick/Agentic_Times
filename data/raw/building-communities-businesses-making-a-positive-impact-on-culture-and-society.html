Building Communities: businesses making a positive impact on culture and society

Date : 2025-10-06T19:21:46Z   |   Auteur : Auteur inconnu   |   Journal : Telstra Best of Business Awards: 2025

Operating in diverse sectors, from gourmet pies to social media, these Australian small and medium-sized businesses are helping their communities thrive.

Here are the businesses that have been named State Winners in the Building Communities category of the 2025 Telstra Best of Business Awards.

Social Protect – Australian Capital Territory winner

Founded to end social media abuse, Social Protect aims to be a source for good in the volatile online landscape. As well as on-demand education, it offers an app designed to detect and automatically delete abusive comments in real time, and provides investigative support so users can stay in the conversation on social media.

　

Camp Quality – New South Wales winner

Camp Quality is a not-for-profit organisation that supports children and families facing cancer. Free of charge, thanks to the support of more than 1,000 volunteers across Australia, it offers programs and services that can be delivered in places that suit families, whether that’s in hospital, at school, at home or at Camp Quality’s retreats and camps.

Saltbush Social Enterprises – Northern Territory winner

Aboriginal organisation Saltbush Social Enterprises provides targeted services to marginalised people living in the Territory. Transforming lives via employment, justice reintegration, training, wellbeing and support programs, it weaves cultural safety, lived experience, and trauma-informed care into the design of its services.

　

Share the Dignity – Queensland winner

Share the Dignity believes everyone deserves a level of dignity that many take for granted. Advocating for menstrual equity, the charity is working to end period poverty by providing essential products for all, including women and girls experiencing homelessness or otherwise doing it tough.

　

Occupational Therapy for Children (OTFC) – South Australian winner

Harnessing the power of play, OTFC is redefining how young people with developmental differences experience therapy. A winner in two categories, this organisation uses purpose-built facilities to support kids with varying diagnoses, behavioural concerns or developmental delays, and is changing the lives of thousands of young Australians.

Lime Finance – Tasmanian winner

Lime Finance is a Launceston-based finance brokerage that aims to take the fear out of borrowing and insurance. It helps people expand their businesses, buy equipment, achieve their personal goals and buy their own homes. By helping people feel supported and understood, it empowers Tasmanians in rural and regional areas to take control of their futures.

Wonder Pies – Victorian winner

Wonder Pies is an innovative food business that supplies handcrafted pies and pastries, made with locally sourced ingredients, to Melburnians. The business supplies hospitals, stadiums, schools and cafes and provides jobs in its Coburg North headquarters, which house a bakery, cafe, store, viewing kitchen and premium events space.

WA All Abilities Football Association – Western Australian winner

The WA All Abilities Football Association (WAAAFA) believes everyone deserves the opportunity to play football. Through its footy programs, WAAAFA actively works towards inclusivity in sport for people living with disabilities, and believes in the power of being part of a team and a wider community.

　

Congratulations to all the State Winners working to help their communities thrive.
