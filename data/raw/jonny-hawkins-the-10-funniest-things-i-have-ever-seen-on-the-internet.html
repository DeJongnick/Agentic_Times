Jonny Hawkins: the 10 funniest things I have ever seen (on the internet)

Date : 2024-09-11T15:00:08Z   |   Auteur : Jonny Hawkins   |   Journal : Culture

When I was asked to submit my 10 funniest things on the internet, I panicked. Will this be the moment people find out I don’t really know how to use a computer? The moment we learn I have no taste? The moment everyone realises how damaging home schooling was for me?

I’m not an “internet person”. I like talking to people, partying and reading. I hate it when someone holds their phone up to me and says, “Watch this.” Or when someone describes something they saw online. I find the internet too noisy. My old housemate used to get offended that I’d not responded to the Instagram posts she’d shared with me. People send me “funny things” all the time and, for the most part, I’m out of touch and I don’t get it. Never have. Like my father before me, just as I grasp a new technology, the world moves on. And I’m left standing there with my redundant relic, asking if I’m cool yet.

This is what home schooling did to me. I wasn’t shaped by a socialised collective consciousness. I’ve never been on-trend in my life. I’ll never truly understand my peers, forever destined to be outside the internet, looking in and wondering why that poorly drawn, shrivelled-up man standing in the corner with the party hat on is at the party at all. I think I’m referencing a meme people know.

I’ve decided to not reach out to friends for their funny suggestions, bypassing the process of my own failure to be fun online. Instead I stand here naked and irrelevant, in the hope that some other humourless luddite might see this and feel better about themselves. It’s possible to be the centre of the party, have a rich inner life and make new friends without being cool online.

Here are my choices of stuff that has made me laugh from my entire history on the internet – all 35 times I’ve used it for something other than emails and sexy videos.

1. The boys are back in town

For my first submission, it’s a tweet from the day I first logged on to Twitter in 2015. It makes me laugh every time i think about. This is a perfect joke.

2. Bowen Yang in Moulin Rouge

This next one wasn’t from the internet. I watched it on TV and looked it up later. I love Bowen Yang and somehow missed the memo on the other girl in the clip, but she seems good too.

3. Homestar Runner

This is a real “if you know, you know” choice. I’m so sorry for alienating everyone here, but Homestar Runner and Strong Bad still have me in stitches. In the early 2000s Homestar Runner was one of the first online cartoons and, for me, was a formative experience. It written by two university lecturers at NYU, The Brothers Chaps, AKA the pioneers of internet animation. Highlights include Trogdor and Different Town but nothing lives on in my brain quite as loudly as this episode: The System Is Down.

4. James McCartney live

This one is hilariously weird. It’s just so, so very weird. James McCartney is trying to be his own musician, asserting his independence from his dad. But the nepo baby’s poisoned chalice is that he will never be out of daddy’s shadow. No father is more keenly, daggily, cringely involved than Sir Paul. Please watch and then rewatch the strange hand kiss. Inarguably the most awkward father-son moment on television. Rock it man, I know you will.

5. Call Me By Your Twins

Twins was a family favourite of mine, Call Me by Your Name was not. This mashup is clearly, cleverly made by a true fan of Twins and it’s impossibly genius.

6. Very Important People

This is a clip from an internet series called Very Important People. The premise is amazing: they give comedians a full makeover, a new costume, makeup, prosthetics, everything, but the comedian has no idea what they look like until the makeover is done. Then the comedians create a new character in 10 minutes. The results are hilarious. I love the way Vic Michaelis holds it all together.

7. Glenn Twenty

Glenn Twenty is a character that a good friend of mine, Duncan Fellows, created. He started out as a DJ character at a local queer bar, The Bearded Tit. Glenn is everybody’s daggy uncle. Over Covid Glenn started making infomercials about washing your hands called Iso-Therapy. And it was just so special. In this video Glenn reviews Wollongong.

8. News anchor farts

A friend of mine once got fired from recording audio books. He didn’t edit the raw records of a certain high-profile journalist’s memoir before publishing. The first four hours of the book were fine but, somewhere in the middle, a few lucky people who downloaded it early got to hear her belching, farting, fumbling words and gossiping with the sound technician (my friend). So I can’t use her name, or post the audio of her book, but this clip just about does the same job. I find farts really funny.

9. The Silence of the Lambs: the romcom

Here’s another one highlighting the clever work of an editor. The premise of this is so stupid but carried out so well. It’s so sinister and cheeky.

10. This church singer

I grew up in a big Pentecostal church. Folks singing, moved by the spirit and testifying about how God made a breakthrough in their life is not unfamiliar to me, but this sister sings with a refreshing, unsanitised honesty that makes me weep. It sounds as though she’s praying for forgiveness but I believe she misses her old life. It sounds like fun.
