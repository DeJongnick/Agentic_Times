Clare Cavanagh: the 10 funniest things I have ever seen (on the internet)

Date : 2024-08-21T15:00:10Z   |   Auteur : Clare Cavanagh   |   Journal : Television & radio

When I was asked to share the 10 funniest things I’ve seen on the internet, I was thrilled and also incredibly smug. I am a comedian with impeccable taste and a patchy work history – I spend plenty of time online consuming and sharing hilarious content with my nearest and dearest! The world (inner-city lefties who read the Guardian) deserves to enjoy my curated content too! How could I possibly whittle down all the witty videos, clever tweets and snarky memes I’ve collected over the years to just 10?

Related: Alison Willmore: the 10 funniest things I have ever seen (on the internet)

Then I looked through my TikTok history and realised there are two categories of content I now consume: women’s football (specifically edits of queer players doing good soccer) and Taylor Swift Reputation theories. Not particularly humorous or broadly appealing.

So I had to work a lot harder than expected on this list. Please enjoy it (and message me if you want any of the aforementioned women’s football and/or Taylor content).

1. Danielle Walker’s password task on Taskmaster Australia

I cried when I watched this. I also watched the whole thing all the way through on the first go, which is so rare for someone like me who has a rotted brain which gets bored after 15 seconds. It’s an incredible journey with pathos, logos and ethos. We get clever editing, Danielle ALMOST guessing the pattern before telling herself that it makes no sense multiple times, Tom breaking as she finally figures it out. Chef’s kiss. No notes. The perfect task.

2. Parks and Recreation – Leslie the Rapper

Look, I don’t want to be too sincere on here but maybe Parks and Rec was the catalyst that got me to drop out of law and pursue the equally highly powered career that is improvised comedy. This is from the episode where Leslie accidentally supports gay marriage because of two gay penguins in the local zoo, but what I love about this cold open is the utter commitment from all of the actors and the stupidity of how long it goes.

3. Zoë Coombs Marr – straight women

I had to have something from Zoë Coombs Marr in this list and I got sucked into a hole basically rewatching her entire special trying to pick my favourite clip. She squashes in so many jokes-per-minute, bends and breaks the fourth wall and also has heaps of lesbian bits which I personally really love. Incidentally, Queerstralia is one of the best pieces of television I’ve ever watched. That’s not this clip but this is my list so I’m sneaking in another recommendation.

4. sheLORDS

My best friend and I like to torment each other by coming up with more and more insane versions of words like “SH-E-O” or “SHEro”. This one is better, because it is a real business with a real website. The final bastion of feminism is making fun of late-stage capitalist feminists or something.

5. The Cats movie

OK, I didn’t see this on the internet, obviously, but I think we’ve sadly let the absolute insanity of this film fall into the annals of history, which is just terrible. I was reminded of this film by a friend’s tweet which maybe includes pirated film footage of the cat behind Judi Dench’s final monologue, so I can’t share it here. But then I looked up the trailer again and my god. It’s perfect. The tails! The faces! The weird proportions! It’s beautiful. They got Sir Ian McKellen and Dame Judi Dench to be in this movie. How?! Hollywood’s hilarious, man.

6. Scrum v Mr Whippy

Scrum is my favourite drag king. He’s a diehard Cronulla Sharks supporter who loves to get his kit off at any chance. His longer stuff is always a witty commentary on sports culture and Aussie masculinity but this clip is just silly and dumb.

7. Catherine Bohart – being friends with your exes

This is a good joke. Catherine Bohart is very funny and she’s also queer – sue me, I like more than one queer female comic! It’s good to remember that you can enjoy funny things even when you don’t necessarily agree with them; I remain friends with all my exes because imagine if someone didn’t like me?!

8. Nina Oyama

Nina makes me laugh, but Nina’s humour is very dirty so there are few tweets I could share with you all. Nina and I actually went to high school together and we still argue over who bullied who. She is now a successful TV comedian, actor and writer and I am an improviser, so I think that probably means it was me?

9. Clare Devlin in Derry Girls

I like compilation videos from my favourite TV shows because they appeal to the part of my brain that can only concentrate for a few minutes at a time but still allow me to watch my silly little programs. I can’t pick my favourite character from Derry Girls, so safe to say I’ve probably watched the entire series via these fan edits. I obviously hold a special place in my heart for Clare Devlin, the wee lesbian, who has one of the best one-liners: “Well I’m not being an individual on my own.”

10. Saturday Night Live’s Super Showcase

“Shonda’s wearing Christopher Kane. And Vonda’s wearing a Christopher Kane knock-off.” To end my list I’ve chosen one of the few things on the internet that is just as funny to watch the 50th time as it was the first time. This sketch still makes me snort and cry.

As a millennial woman in comedy it is the law that I love Kristen Wiig and Maya Rudolph, and it is also the law that any time this sketch pops up I rewatch it in full. I actually tossed up between this and Wiig’s Liza Minnelli Tries to Turn Off a Lamp, but since Super Showcase is a Maya-Kristen double act with the most committed straight-woman in Vanessa Bayer, I picked this. But please, if you haven’t seen Liza Minnelli Tries to Turn Off a Lamp I need you to Google it immediately. Please.
