A country retreat that’s off-grid but online

Date : 2025-10-12T16:05:12Z   |   Auteur : Auteur inconnu   |   Journal : Travel

Your article (‘I step outside into a cacophony of nature’: an off-grid escape in the west of England, 11 October) sings the praises of a cottage that, on its website, boasts about the fast broadband, copious USB points and good mobile phone signal. Hardly “off-grid” then.
Janet Thompson
West Runton, Norfolk

• Like Bob Brody (‘Stay true to yourself – and fly closer to the sun’: what I’ve learned from 50 years of rejection, 8 October), I’ve published two books and over a thousand articles, but had many more rejections – in my case, in the academic field. The ratio is much worse for my grant applications. I advise young researchers to cultivate a thick skin and a short memory.
Prof Mark Woodward
Drummoyne, New South Wales, Australia

• Re using the radio to help with dozing off (Letters, 10 October), Radio 4’s Today in Parliament at 11.30pm is very useful: “Order, order…” works almost immediately. If parliament is not sitting, In Our Time, available on demand, also works a treat.
Tim Martineau
Prenton, Merseyside

• My favourite mondegreen (Letters, 3 October) was from an interview with Andy Murray following a grand slam victory. The interviewer asked: “How do you feel after that match, Andy?” The subtitle read: “How do you feel after that much brandy?”
Richard Williams
Hove, East Sussex
