Accelerating Women: businesses closing the gender gap

Date : 2025-10-06T19:20:24Z   |   Auteur : Auteur inconnu   |   Journal : Telstra Best of Business Awards: 2025

To create equity for women, businesses need to challenge exclusion, reshape male-dominated industries, help women remain employed or re-enter the workforce, provide pathways to leadership positions and insist on equal pay.

Here are the Australian small and medium-sized businesses that have taken some of these actions and been named State Winners in the Accelerating Women category of the 2025 Telstra Best of Business Awards.

18FIFTY3 GROUP – Australian Capital Territory winner

The 18FIFTY3 GROUP prides itself on having a diverse group of team members, and celebrating women; it was named in honour of the founder’s great, great-grandmother, born in 1853. The business delivers integrated services across civil construction, land management, environmental consulting, modular housing and vocational training. By blending social purpose with technical excellence, 18FIFTY3 GROUP aims to create lasting, meaningful change.

Elevated Coaching & Consulting – New South Wales winner

This small NSW business offers leadership development, diversity and inclusion, and workforce wellbeing programs. Female-founded and led, Elevated Coaching & Consulting provides bespoke business training that helps organisations tackle psychosocial risks, improve equity outcomes and boost employee performance and resilience.

BEME MOVEMENT EDUCATION (BeME) – Northern Territory winner

A love of movement, education and personal growth come together at BeME. It delivers trauma-aware, culturally responsive movement programs to help harness young people’s energy in remote communities across the Northern Territory and beyond. Through curiosity, breath, and high-energy games, BeME helps young people regulate, focus and connect, building the physical literacy and emotional safety that underpin learning.

Resource Hub – Queensland winner

Founded in Brisbane and proudly led by women, Resource Hub works with councils and waste operators to solve essential everyday waste management challenges. It delivers specialist, hands-on support to bridge the divide between regulation and real-world site operations.

Continence Matters – South Australian winner

Continence Matters was established in 2002 by Dr Samantha Pillay OAM, the first woman to complete adult urology surgical training in South Australia. It provides resources to help clinical teams offer care and treatment to people with disabling bladder conditions and works to promote a wider acceptance and understanding of these conditions within the community.

EPIC – Tasmanian winner

EPIC (Empowering People in Construction) is a social impact business dedicated to transforming the culture within the traditionally male-dominated construction industry. To make construction a more inclusive space, EPIC delivers people-focused services including peer support, mentoring, coaching and training workshops to address inequity, discrimination, mental health risks and systemic challenges.

Rahma Health – Victorian winner

Also a winner in the Championing Health category, Rahma Health is a Melbourne charity that provides culturally and psychologically safe parenting and health resources for Arabic-speaking families, particularly those affected by war, displacement, racism, poverty and intergenerational trauma. Its healthcare information is written and reviewed by its team of health professionals, which includes GPs, obstetricians, speech pathologists, dentists, midwives and nurses.

Pregnancy to Parenthood Clinic (P2P) – Western Australian winner

The Pregnancy to Parenthood Clinic (P2P) is a community-based early intervention service for families facing emotional challenges during the transition to parenthood. Providing free support from pregnancy to early childhood (up to age three), P2P strengthens emotional wellbeing and nurtures parent-infant relationships, in a bid to ensure every child and family thrives from the start.

Congratulations to all the deserving State Winners making equity their priority.
