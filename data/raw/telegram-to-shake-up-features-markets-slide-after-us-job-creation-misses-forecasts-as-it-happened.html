Newsflash: Britain‚Äôs competition authority believes Google is using anti-competitive practices in the market for adverts on websites and apps, potentially harming thousands of UK publishers and advertisers.

Following an investigation, the Competition and Markets Authority has provisionally found that Google has abused its dominant positions through the operation of both its publisher ad server and buying tools to restrict competition in the UK.

The CMA says Google ‚Äòself-preferences‚Äô its own ad exchange - harming competition and, as a result, advertisers and publishers.

This relates to the rapid series of auctions and transactions which takes place when a user opens a web page or app, to determine which ads will be shown.

The CMA accuses Google of abusing its dominant position to strengthen the market position of its AdX service ‚Äì an ad exchange that matches bids from publishers and advertisers.

Google also operates ad buying tools for publishers, and for advertisers, and the CMA believes it has conducted various practices that give AdX competitive advantages, and disadvantage Google‚Äôs rivals.

They include:

  • providing AdX with exclusive or preferential access to advertisers that use Google Ads‚Äô platform;

  • manipulating advertiser bids so that they have a higher value when submitted into AdX‚Äôs auction than when submitted into rival exchanges‚Äô auctions; and

  • allowing AdX to bid first in auctions run by DFP for online advertising space, effectively giving it an ‚Äòright of first refusal‚Äô - with rivals potentially not having any chance to submit bids.

The regulator has laid out its concerns in a statement of objections today.

Juliette Enser, interim executive director of enforcement at the CMA, explains:

We‚Äôve provisionally found that Google is using its market power to hinder competition when it comes to the ads people see on websites.
