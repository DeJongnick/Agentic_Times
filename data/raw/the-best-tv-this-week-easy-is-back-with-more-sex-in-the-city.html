The best TV this week: Easy is back with more sex in the city

Date : 2017-11-24T12:00:04Z   |   Auteur : The Guide   |   Journal : Television & radio

Easy

More tales from the city – in this case Chicago – as Joe Swanberg’s comedy-drama anthology series returns. Most of season one’s more memorable characters return, including Dave Franco’s scam artist Jeff, and Kyle and Andi (Michael Chernus and Elizabeth Reaser), who are considering an open marriage.
Available from 1 December, Netflix

The Robot Will See You Now

A whole week of bot-related programming from Channel 4 that explores sex-bots, driver-bots and, in the case of this documentary, empathy-bots. A glimpse of what’s looking like a weird future.
28 November, 10pm, Channel 4

Dark

“Yesterday, today and tomorrow are not consecutive.” This enigmatic statement is a clue to where Netflix’s first German original is going. Shades of French mystery Les Revenants as the disappearance of two children gives rise to all manner of temporal bewilderment.
Available from 1 December, Netflix

Joe Orton Laid Bare

The 1967 murder of the playwright Orton – by his partner Kenneth Halliwell – robbed us of a thrilling, flourishing literary talent. Fifty years on, this documentary looks back on Orton’s sadly brief career and airs an unpublished “pornographic” playlet for the first time.
25 November, 9pm, BBC Two

The X Factor

The singing contest-cum-Syco marketing campaign continues. Rak-Su remain favourites but Kevin Davy White will want a say, too.
25 November, 7.30pm, ITV

Babylon Berlin

We’ve reached the end of the first season of this impressive interwar drama. But don’t worry: in defiance of all known showbiz traditions, Sky isn’t making us wait for season two; it begins at the same time next week. For now, Rath and Bruno finally have a lead on the missing film reel but, alongside the truth, there will almost certainly be consequences.
26 November, 9pm, Sky Atlantic

Man Down

Greg Davies’s gift for the pratfall and taste for the grotesque has made him a physical comedian almost on a par with his hero and in-show father, Rik Mayall. As the fourth season of his irresistible sitcom draws to a close, Dan is desperate to prove his credentials as a dad. We all know that’s not going to happen, right?
29 November, 10pm, Channel 4

Storyville: Last Men in Aleppo

This film won the grand jury documentary prize at the 2017 Sundance festival, and no wonder: it’s a startling testimony to the heroic efforts of Syrian volunteer rescue organisation the White Helmets to salvage human life from Aleppo’s rubble.
27 November, 10pm, BBC Four

Chinese Burns

Comedy pilots on BBC Three aren’t always a byword for consistency, but this new sitcom might break the mould. Written by and starring Yennis Cheung and Shin-Fei Chen, it follows the escapades of three young Chinese women, new in London and leaping aboard the capital’s merry-go-round of binge-drinking, rent-struggling and relationship-puzzling.
Available from 27 November, BBC Three

Witnesses: A Frozen Death

The second series of the French crime-mystery thriller. An amnesiac is linked to 15 dead men found frozen on board an abandoned bus.
25 November, 9pm, BBC Four
